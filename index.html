Indian Economy on the Eve of Independence

Azaadi se pehle Bharat ki arthvyavastha kaisi thi? British rule ne kya tabahi ki? Poora chapter simple Hindi-English mein — Board exam ke liye 100% ready!

⚠️ Exam Tip: Is chapter se har saal MCQ, 3-mark aur 4-mark dono types ke questions aate hain. Per capita income ₹230, 80% agriculture dependence, aur 5 national income estimators — ye teen cheezein zaroor yaad rakhein!
1.1 Overall Features of Indian Economy — Eve of Independence
📉
(a) Stagnant Economy
  • Economy mein little or no growth in income
  • National income ka sarkari andaaza kabhi nahi lagaya gaya
  • 20th century ke first half mein real output growth < 2%
  • Per capita output growth — sirf 0.5% per year
  • Epidemics aur famines baar baar hote the
  • Standard of living miserably low tha
💸
(b) Backward Economy
  • 1947-48 mein per capita income sirf ₹230
  • Very low per capita income = backward economy
  • Log khana, kapda, makaan se vanchit the
  • Widespread unemployment tha
  • Handicraft industries ke naash se poverty badhaa
🌾
(c) Dominance of Agriculture
  • ~80% population — directly/indirectly agriculture pe dependent
  • ~70% working population — agriculture mein engaged
  • Famine baar baar hoti thi
  • Output per acre (productivity) bahut kam tha
  • Food shortage aur starvation deaths common thi
🏭
(d) Bleak Industrialisation
  • Industrial development bahut dheema tha
  • British policies ne Indian handicrafts destroy kiye
  • Modern industries ki growth extremely slow thi
  • Machinery aur capital goods import karne padte the
  • Small-scale industries buri tarah se tabaah ho gayi
🌍
(e) Heavy Dependence on Imports
  • Machinery, medicines, kerosene oil, chemicals import hote the
  • Defence equipment bhi bahar se aata tha
  • Indian industries etni kamzor ki ye sab bana nahi sakti thi
  • Country heavily dependent on foreign imports thi
🏙️
(f) Limited Urbanisation
  • Zyaadatar log gaanv mein rehte the
  • 1948 mein sirf ~15% urban population
  • Agriculture ke bahar employment bahut kam thi
  • Urban facilities bahut limited thi
🏚️
(g) Semi-Feudal Economy
  • Zamindars ke paas zameen — farmers mazloom
  • Neither wholly capitalist nor wholly feudal
  • Feudalistic mode of production dominant tha
  • Purane tarike — low productivity
  • Farmers ke paas koi rights nahi the
1.1.1 India Before British Rule
🪙
Samridh Bharat (Pre-British Era)
  • India was a vibrant and prosperous country
  • Agriculture mukhya peshaa — gaanv self-sufficient
  • Handicrafts aur cotton textiles — world-famous
  • Silk, metal products, fine craftsmanship globally known
  • Indian goods puri duniya mein export hote the
  • Sona-chandi trade ke zariye India aata tha
1.1.2 Exploitation & Transformation During British Rule
⚙️
(i) Supply Side Exploitation
  • India = raw material ka supplier for Britain
  • Cotton, jute, indigo etc. export hote the
  • British industries ne Indian raw material use kiya
  • India ki resources Britain ke liye extract ki gayi
🛒
(ii) Demand Side Exploitation
  • India bana British finished goods ka market
  • Indians ko British-made goods kharidni padte the
  • Indian handicraft industry completely destroyed
  • Indian markets were used as 'demand destination'
😔
Effects on Indian Economy
  • Indian handicraft industries destroyed
  • Farmers heavily taxed + land se ejected
  • Agriculture stopped producing surplus
  • Poverty aur unemployment bahut badhaa
  • Economy became weak, backward, dependent
  • Railways bhi colonial interest ke liye bani — Indian economy ke liye nahi
1.2 Agricultural Sector on the Eve of Independence
📉
(a) Low Production & Low Productivity
  • Production = total output; Productivity = output per hectare
  • Purane aur primitive farming methods
  • Poor irrigation facilities
  • Modern tools aur technology nahi thi
  • Farmers ke paas invest karne ke paise nahi
🏗️
(b) Exploitative Land Settlement
  • Zamindari system — bahut exploitative
  • Zamindars ne farmers se zyaada revenue collect kiya
  • Farmers ko land tenure ki koi security nahi
  • Produce ka bada hissa zamindar + British le lete
  • Farmers ke paas reinvest karne ke liye kuch nahi bachta
🌿
(c) Forced Commercialisation
  • Farmers ko cash crops ugaane par majboor kiya
  • Indigo, cotton, jute — importance di gayi
  • Food crop production neglect hua
  • Farmers ko apna produce low prices pe bechna pada
  • Food shortage aur akaal badhay
💧
(d) Lack of Investment in Infrastructure
  • Irrigation bahut poor tha
  • Transport aur storage facilities inadequate
  • Institutional credit ka koi source nahi
  • British ne agriculture mein invest nahi kiya
  • Little investment by British government in agriculture
👨‍🌾
(e) Gap Between Owners & Tillers
  • Zamindars (owners) ke paas zameen — tillers kaam karte
  • Farmers ko produce ka chhota hissa milta tha
  • Improve karne ka koi motivation nahi
  • Gulf between owners and tillers of soil
🗺️
(f) Small & Fragmented Holdings
  • Land holdings bahut chhote aur bikhar hue
  • Small farms = low efficiency
  • Modern methods apply karna mushkil
  • Economies of scale nahi thi
🏠
(g) Subsistence Outlook
  • Farmers mainly apne liye produce karte the
  • Marketable surplus bahut kam tha
  • Commercial agriculture develop nahi ho saki
  • Farmers produced mainly for self-consumption

❓ Board Exam Important Questions with Answers
Q1. British colonial rule ka sole purpose India mein kya tha?
Ans: British rule ka ek hi maqsad tha — India ko (i) raw material supplier banana aur (ii) Indian market ko British finished goods ka destination banana. Ye pure colonial exploitation thi. Railways bhi Indian economy ke liye nahi, colonial interest ke liye bani thi.
Q2. Stagnant economy aur Backward economy mein kya antar hai?
Ans: Stagnant economy = aisi economy jisme aay mein little or no growth ho (rate ka issue). Backward economy = aisi economy jisme per capita income bahut low ho (level ka issue). 1947 mein India dono tha — income grow nahi rahi thi aur level bhi ₹230 jitna low tha.
Q3. Independence ki sandhya pe agriculture ki 4 main features kya thin?
Ans: (i) Low production aur productivity — purane methods, poor irrigation. (ii) Exploitative land settlement — Zamindari system. (iii) Forced commercialisation — cash crops ugaane ka dbaav. (iv) Lack of investment in infrastructure — irrigation, storage, credit sab absent. Additional features: small & fragmented holdings, gap between owners & tillers, subsistence outlook.
Q4. Colonial exploitation ke do paksh (Supply & Demand) explain karo.
Ans: (i) Supply side — India British industries ko raw material supply karta tha (cotton, jute, indigo). (ii) Demand side — India British finished goods ka market ban gaya. Indians ko British-made goods kharidni padte the. Is dual exploitation ne Indian economy ko hollow kar diya.
